Research

My research asks: How can quantitative soft-matter physics and transport phenomena be used to build predictive models of human tissue function and disease? I integrate interfacial mechanics, imaging/analysis, and microphysiological systems engineering to control geometry, flow, matrix mechanics, and transport—then connect these variables to inflammation, immune dynamics, and therapeutic response.

Program structure

  • Core science: mechanobiology at physiological interfaces — quantify how shear, curvature, matrix compliance, and transport shape tissue behavior.
  • Translational engine: predictive pulmonary platforms for therapy discovery — autologous, immune-competent airway models coupled to functional readouts and multi-omic profiling.

Selected projects

1) Electrowetting contact-line dynamics (PhD)

Quantified how microscopic contact-line dissipation controls macroscopic droplet transport under electrowetting, providing design rules for robust droplet motion and vertical detachment in digital microfluidics.

  • Methods: high-speed imaging, image analysis, modeling
  • Representative outputs: PRL / PRE / Sci Rep

2) Active elimination of lubricated air films (dielectrophoresis)

Developed a spatially periodic dielectrophoretic strategy to eliminate thin air films trapped between a liquid and a solid, enabling bubble-free casting/coating and improving interfacial heat transfer performance.

  • Methods: periodic DEP fields, high-speed + ultra-fast imaging
  • Representative outputs: Nat. Commun. / Thermal Science & Engineering Progress

3) Fast wetting of soft solids: unsteady ridge formation

Built high-speed interferometric imaging to resolve early-time wetting ridge dynamics on compliant substrates, revealing deformation anisotropy and its consequences for wetting kinetics and stick–slip behaviors.

  • Methods: phase-shifting interferometry, ultra-high-speed imaging
  • Representative outputs: JCIS + optical metrology/patent work

4) ECM.MV.Chip — matrix-embedded circular microvasculature-on-chip

Engineered a 3D circular, matrix-embedded microvasculature-on-chip that sustains physiologically relevant shear distributions, enabling controlled studies of endothelial mechanotransduction within ECM and stromal co-culture contexts.

  • Methods: microfabrication, perfusion control, ECM design, human vascular co-culture
  • Representative output: Advanced Functional Materials (cover feature)

Figure: ECM.MV.Chip enabling controlled shear + ECM microenvironment for vascular mechanobiology.

5) Autologous airway-on-chip for Down syndrome immune dysfunction

Developing an autologous iPSC-derived small-airway-on-chip integrating donor-matched epithelial, endothelial, stromal, and immune components. Using matched disomic vs trisomic systems to study baseline immune hyper-responsiveness and exaggerated inflammatory/viral responses, enabling therapeutic discovery pipelines.

  • Methods: iPSC differentiation, immune-competent co-culture, controlled mechanics/transport, high-content profiling
  • Representative outputs: organ-on-chip platform IP + manuscripts in preparation

Figure: Genetically defined, immune-competent airway-on-chip for mechanistic disease modeling and therapy testing.

Physics → Platforms → Therapeutics (workflow)

  1. Engineer controlled geometry/flow/matrix mechanics
  2. Perturb tissues (infection, cytokines, mechanics, drugs)
  3. Measure function (barrier, cilia, imaging) + multi-omics
  4. Infer actionable nodes with interpretable models
  5. Validate with on-chip perturbation experiments
